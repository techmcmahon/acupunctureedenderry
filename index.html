What is Acupuncture ?


Acupuncture is an ancient system of healing developed over thousands of years as part of the traditional medicine of China, Japan and other Eastern countries. It has evolved into a system of medicine that restores and maintains health by the insertion to fine needles into acupuncture points just beneath the body surfaces. These points are in very specific locations and lie on channels of energy. I use pre-sterilised, disposable needles that are single use only and as a member of The Traditional Chinese Medicine Council of Ireland, a recognised fully qualified professional practitioner. It is a condition of membership that each member carries Professional Indemnity Insurance and Public Liability Insurance cover.


Commonly Treated Conditions

The World Health Organisation recognises Acupuncture and Traditional Chinese Medicine as a viable means of treatment for a wide range of conditions.
Some of the most commonly seen complaints in the acupuncture clinic are as follows:

  • Infections: Colds, Flu, Viral Infections, Ear, Nose and Throat conditions: Sinusitis, Hayfever, Allergic Rhinitis, Catarrh.
  • Dermatology: Eczema, Psoriasis, Acne.
  • Psychological: Depression, Stress, Insomnia, Anxiety.
  • Musculoskeletal: Rheumatoid Arthritis, Periarthritis of Shoulder, Sciatica, Back Pain, Sports Injury, Neck Pain, Bells Palsy, Tennis Elbow, Knee Pain, Facial Pain, Dental Pain, TMJ Syndrome.
  • Internal Disease: Asthma, Bronchitis, Blood Pressure, Indigestion, Bowel Problems, Diabetes, M.E, Headaches / Migraine, M.S. Trigerninal Neuralgia, Peptic Ulcer, Stroke, Dysentry, Biliary Colic, Renal Colic, Adverse Reaction to Radiation and Chemotherapy Treatments, Acute and Chronic Gastritis, Genitourinary / Gynaecology: Infertility, Impotence, Premenstrual Syndrome, Menstrual Problems, Menopause, Cystitis, Induction of Labour, Morning Sickness, Dysmenorhea.
